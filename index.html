The Red Planet

Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun and the second-smallest planet in the Solar System, being larger than only Mercury.

The Gas Giant

Jupiter is the fifth planet from the Sun and the largest in the Solar System. It is a gas giant with a mass more than two and a half times that of all the other planets combined.

The Ringed Planet

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and the second-largest in the Solar System, after Jupiter. It is a gas giant with an average radius of about nine and a half times that of Earth.

Space Facts

The Universe is Expanding

The universe is continuously expanding, and the rate of expansion is accelerating. This discovery led to the theory of dark energy, which makes up about 68% of the universe.

There are More Stars than Grains of Sand on Earth

There are approximately 100 billion stars in our galaxy (the Milky Way) alone, and there are approximately 100 billion galaxies in the observable universe.

A Day on Venus is Longer than a Year

Venus rotates so slowly that it takes 243 Earth days to complete one rotation. However, it only takes 225 Earth days to orbit the Sun once.
